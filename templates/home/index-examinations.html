MRI 3T

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) at 3 Tesla is a more powerful version of the MRI technology, producing higher resolution images with greater clarity and detail, making it particularly useful in detecting subtle abnormalities, assessing cardiac function, and evaluating musculoskeletal conditions.

MRI 1.5T

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) at 1.5 Tesla is a non-invasive imaging technique that uses a magnetic field and radio waves to create detailed images of the body's organs and tissues, providing valuable information for diagnosing conditions such as tumors, brain disorders, and joint injuries.

Computed Tomography

Computed Tomography (CT) is a medical imaging technique that uses X-rays and computer processing to generate cross-sectional images of the body. It is commonly used to diagnose and monitor conditions such as bone fractures, internal bleeding, and cancer, offering quick and detailed information about the body's internal structures.

X-Ray

X-ray imaging is a widely used diagnostic technique that utilizes low-dose radiation to create images of the body's internal structures, especially bones. It is commonly used to diagnose fractures, lung infections, and dental issues, providing a quick and accessible way to visualize abnormalities.

Mammography

Mammography is a specialized X-ray imaging technique used to screen and diagnose breast cancer. It involves compressing the breast between two plates and capturing detailed images to detect early signs of breast abnormalities, allowing for early detection and improved treatment outcomes.

Ultrasound

Ultrasound imaging, also known as sonography, uses high-frequency sound waves to produce real-time images of the body's organs and tissues. It is commonly used for prenatal care to monitor fetal development, as well as for diagnosing conditions in the abdomen, pelvis, and vascular system, offering a safe and non-invasive method for imaging various body regions.
